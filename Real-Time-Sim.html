ABSORPTION

Gas absorption is an operation in which a gas mixture is contacted with a liquid for the purposes of preferentially dissolving one or more components of the gas and to provide a solution of them in the liquid. When mass transfer occurs in the opposite direction, i.e. from the liquid to the gas, the operation is called desorption, or stripping. The principles of both absorption and desorption are basically the same.
